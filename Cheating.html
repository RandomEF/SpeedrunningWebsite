Cheating

However, good things will always have a pinch of salt with them. Cheating is quite a big box of salt, and moderators try extremely hard to stop any cheated videos from leaking into the record stream.

There are many things moderators do to catch cheated runs:

  • Analyzing the audio in a spectogram
  • Probabilty checks
  • Integrity of game files
  • Heavy lag (large gaps in subsequent frames) in the video
  • Change in parts of a game e.g. the physics
  • The run being in line with the rules of the category
  • Comparison to previous records
  • Checking the inputs/input methods

It's like airport security. There are many layers of checking to stop illegal items from getting through.

Nonetheless, some still get through. For example, in Trackmania, Riolu, a top player, was found to have cheated thousands of his records, even though he can prove his worth. Or how in Minecraft, Dream, 'accidentally' left some game mods enabled in his world record run, that improved the probabilities for him. This seems to be a recurring theme, where the best of the best will be the ones who end up cheating the most. Riolu gave his own personal view - he felt that since he was such a top-level runner, he deserved to have the record or those few milliseconds to set a new one and the fact that he was unable to get even after so many attempts was enough to push him past the edge and caused him to start cheating.

Alas once he was caught, there were serious repercussions, as there always is for cheaters. Bans on submissions are enacted to cheaters, perhaps fines. All to dissuade the act of cheating.
